# UI Design Workflow Breakdown — Online Course Platform Landing Page
WORKFLOW ==> design-profile-online-course

## 1) Design Summary
- Style: modern, playful, educational, conversion-focused.
- Layout: hero section, category chips, promotional feature cards, popular course grid.
- Visual tone: light, clean, friendly, optimistic, skill-building oriented.
- Goal: attract learners, showcase course variety, and push signups or course views.

## 2) Typography
- Use a bold sans-serif for main headings and a clean sans-serif for supporting text.
- Headings: large, confident, stacked, and easy to scan.
- Body copy: short, supportive, and benefit-led.
- CTA text: direct, motivating, and learning-oriented.

## 3) Color Direction
- Base palette: white, soft lavender, light gray, and dark navy.
- Accent colors: teal, mint, pastel yellow, pink, and light blue.
- Use pastel chips and cards to make categories feel approachable.
- Keep contrast strong in headlines and CTAs for clarity.

## 4) Layout Structure
- Top navigation with logo, courses, pages, blog, contact, login, and cart/search icons.
- Hero section with large headline, CTA button, and learner imagery.
- Category chip row for quick topic discovery.
- Two promotional feature cards for teachers and individual learners.
- Popular courses section with filters and course cards.
- Optional bottom CTA or further category exploration area.

## 5) Components
- Header navigation with account and utility icons.
- Hero banner with headline, subtext, and primary CTA.
- Enrolled student stat card or social proof module.
- Rounded category pills with icons.
- Dual feature cards with image accents and CTA buttons.
- Course cards with tags, instructor, title, lessons, price, and details link.
- Wishlist or favorite icon on course thumbnails.

## 6) Imagery Direction
- Photography style: real students, learning sessions, laptops, notebooks, and instructor scenes.
- Background treatment: bright studio lighting, minimal distractions, soft gradients.
- Composition: large hero subject on one side, content blocks on the other.
- Prompt style: “modern online education platform, students learning with laptops, pastel UI, clean e-learning landing page”.

## 7) Design System Notes
- Keep spacing airy and balanced for readability.
- Use rounded cards, soft shadows, and subtle borders.
- Make category chips feel tactile and easy to tap.
- Maintain consistent button styling across sections.
- Prioritize browse, enroll, and explore actions throughout the page.

## 8) Follow-Up Upgrade Suggestions
- Add filters for subject, skill level, price, and duration.
- Include instructor profiles and ratings.
- Add enrollment progress, badges, or certification indicators.
- Support dark mode for extended browsing.
- Add a featured learning path or roadmap section.
- Optimize mobile with swipeable category chips and stacked course cards.
