# UI Design Workflow Breakdown — Luxury Resort Landing Page
WORKFLOW ==> design-profile-luxury-resort

## 1) Design Summary
- Style: elegant, premium, warm, hospitality-focused.
- Layout: cinematic hero, booking CTA, service info bar, room card grid, refined discovery section.
- Visual tone: dark brown, gold accents, ambient lighting, high-end comfort.
- Goal: communicate luxury and encourage stay reservations.

## 2) Typography
- Use a refined serif for headlines and a clean sans-serif for supporting text.
- Headings: large, elegant, spaced for a premium feel.
- Body copy: minimal, soft, and descriptive.
- CTA text: short, confident, and reservation-focused.

## 3) Color Direction
- Base palette: deep brown, charcoal, beige, cream, and warm gold.
- Accent colors: muted gold and soft amber for buttons and highlights.
- Keep contrast rich but controlled to preserve the upscale mood.
- Avoid bright colors that break the luxury atmosphere.

## 4) Layout Structure
- Top navigation with logo, links, and booking button.
- Full-width hero section with hotel room imagery and primary CTA.
- Service strip for check-in, location, and support details.
- Room category cards with images, descriptions, and pricing.
- Discovery section with editorial copy and supporting visuals.
- Strong footer or secondary booking area for conversion.

## 5) Components
- Header navigation with highlighted reserve button.
- Hero banner with headline, subtitle, and “Book Your Stay” CTA.
- Info chips for check-in/out, service location, and customer support.
- Room cards with image, title, short text, and price button.
- Discovery section with narrative copy and supporting imagery.
- Secondary CTA for deeper browsing or booking.

## 6) Imagery Direction
- Photography style: luxurious interiors, soft lamps, warm lighting, clean compositions.
- Background treatment: dark atmospheric textures with polished bedroom scenes.
- Composition: wide hero shot, centered bedding, and elegant room details.
- Prompt style: “luxury resort interior, warm cinematic lighting, premium hospitality branding, elegant hotel suite”.

## 7) Design System Notes
- Use generous spacing to reinforce calm and comfort.
- Rounded buttons and subtle shadows should feel soft, not modern-tech.
- Keep cards dark with gold callouts for consistency.
- Maintain clear hierarchy between booking actions and room content.
- Ensure image consistency across room categories.

## 8) Follow-Up Upgrade Suggestions
- Add room comparison filters by price, view, and occupancy.
- Include a booking availability calendar.
- Add guest reviews and star ratings for social proof.
- Show spa, dining, and amenities highlights.
- Add animated transitions for room browsing.
- Optimize mobile with sticky booking CTA and stacked room cards.
