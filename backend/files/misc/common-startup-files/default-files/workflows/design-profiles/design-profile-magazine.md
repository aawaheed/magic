# UI Design Workflow Breakdown — Magazine News Homepage
WORKFLOW ==> design-profile-magazine

## 1) Design Summary
- Style: editorial, clean, modern, content-first.
- Layout: strong hero story, side latest feed, multi-column category grids.
- Visual tone: bright, airy, structured, high readability.
- Goal: surface top stories fast and encourage deeper article exploration.

## 2) Typography
- Use a bold serif or strong sans-serif for headlines.
- Body copy: compact, readable, and article-friendly.
- Metadata: small, muted, uppercase or label-style hierarchy.
- Section titles should stay crisp and easy to scan.

## 3) Color Direction
- Base palette: white, black, soft gray, and muted blue accents.
- Use category labels to differentiate content sections.
- Keep emphasis restrained to maintain an editorial look.
- Highlight featured stories with stronger contrast and larger image blocks.

## 4) Layout Structure
- Top navigation with logo, categories, search, and subscribe button.
- Large featured story block in the center.
- Latest stories sidebar for quick updates.
- Category sections like business, travel, and politics in card grids.
- Large lead article block near the lower fold for emphasis.
- Footer area should remain minimal and content-supportive.

## 5) Components
- Header navigation with category links and subscription CTA.
- Hero story with large image, category label, headline, and excerpt.
- Latest list widget with small thumbnails and compact text.
- Article cards with image, tag, title, date, and summary.
- Featured story banner with stronger visual weight.
- Author/date metadata and optional excerpt preview.

## 6) Imagery Direction
- Photography style: real-world editorial, documentary, lifestyle, and news imagery.
- Background treatment: natural environments, urban scenes, and authentic moments.
- Composition: mix of large lead visuals and smaller supporting thumbnails.
- Prompt style: “modern editorial news photography, clean magazine homepage, natural light, high readability”.

## 7) Design System Notes
- Keep spacing generous for readability and visual balance.
- Use thin dividers and subtle borders to separate sections.
- Shadows should be minimal or absent for a print-like feel.
- Maintain consistent image ratios across article cards.
- Preserve clear hierarchy between featured, latest, and secondary content.

## 8) Follow-Up Upgrade Suggestions
- Add trending tags or most-read modules.
- Include infinite scroll or pagination for long feeds.
- Add hover states with quick article previews.
- Support dark mode for late-night reading.
- Add author pages and topic archives for deeper navigation.
- Optimize mobile with stacked cards and collapsible category menus.
